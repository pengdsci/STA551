ASSIGNMENT #6 (Optional): Although this assignment is optional, I still encourage you to consider these two unsupervised methods in your analysis if they are appropriate to your project.
  • Clustering analysis
    • If your data size is large, clustering algorithms may have potential computational issues. I don't encourage you to do clustering.
    • If your data size is relatively small, you can all numerical feature variables to see whether the clustering methods can find obvious clusters in the data and add the cluster ID to the data and include it in your original data to improve the performance of your modeling.
  • Principal component analysis
    • If your data set has several correlated numerical feature variables, you may want to use PCA to see whether you can find fewer components to include in modeling and reduce the total number of feature variables.
    • PCA is a dimension reduction method, but this does not mean that PCA will always reduce the number of feature variables in the model.

Topic 10: Data Science Consulting Practicum: HR Analytics


TOPICS: HR analytics: Data Science Consulting Projects

HR analytics is a data-driven approach to managing human resources that involves gathering and analyzing data related to employees to derive insights and make informed decisions.

Two project teams will be formed and each team will address three practical questions listed below.
  • Practical Questions for Team #1 (Dragonetti, Echefu, Heneghan, Kuo)
    • Identify departments with high employee turnover and the potential factors.
    • Identified key factors affecting job satisfaction.
    • Provide recommendations to improve employee attrition and job satisfaction.
  • Practical Questions for Team #2 (Lama, Moffitt, Reaves, Taylor, Tomko)
    • Identified key predictors of employees' performance and the potential factors.
    • Identify departments with low engagement levels and the key drivers.
    • Provide recommendations to improve employee performance and engagement.
  • The data set contains information from the Human Resource Information System (employee demographic information, employment history, and compensation details), Performance Management System (employee performance ratings, goals, and achievements), Employee Surveys (feedback on job satisfaction, work-life balance, and engagement), and Exit Interviews (reasons for employee departures and feedback on their experiences). The combined clean data set in csv format is available at | HR Analytic Data Set|

  • Each team will have two 4'x8' size white boards to record your team's plan of formulation of analytic questions, data preparation, feature engineering, modeling (including those you learned in other classes), the goodness of your methodologies, limitations and improvements, some thoughts of implementation, and recommendations to your HR clients.
